3D Printing

The Goal: Assemble and level 3D printer and create (in Rhino), slice (in Cura) a series of objects to print, including a nested set of objects.

Tools Used: Illustrator, Rhino, Calipers, Filament, 3D Printer, many YouTube tutorials on how to print things on an Ender3 Pro

Setting Up the Printer

I began by setting up the printer following the instructions included with the model. The printer is a Creality Ender3 Pro and took approximately 2.5 hours to assemble. After assembly I loaded the filament and got to work leveling the bed. For this I used the instuctions found on Thingiverse here: https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:3235018 along with the YouTube video linked therein. This process was very tedious and took approximately 45 minutes of adjusting. I also had to change the Z height in order to make the glass plate fit onto the bed.

First Prints

I printed the following test prints. My first test print was a massive failure and I had to go back to the drawing board and re-level the bed again. I then created a cube in rhino and exporting it as an STL into Cura and then using the microSD chip to transfer the file to the Ender3 Pro. I used the same process for all of the prints that I made.

A 2cm cube with the standard "low quality" settings (25 minutes to print) A 2cm cube with the standard "standard quality" settings (25 minutes to print) A 2cm cube with the standard "super quality" settings (50 minutes to print)

I checked the final measurements of the cube and they were very close to the intended size. I then printed a 2 cm cube with a concentric top and bottom layer and with the "super quality" setting as that was the setting that I liked the most in the test prints. Time to print for that was 46 minutes.

My settings were placed at 200 degrees for the nozzle and the bed was set to 60 degrees fahrenheit.

Finally, I printed the following items and here are their print times:

A tube 3cm in diameter and 3cm high with a single extrusion wall thickness (20 minutes to print) A tube 3cm in diameter and 3cm high with a double extrusion wall thickness and random z-seam alignment (24 minutes to print) A cylinder 3cm in diameter exported with a 0.1mm tolerance (44 minutes to print) A cylinder 3cm in diameter exported with a 0.001mm tolerance (42 minutes to print) A cylinder 3cm in diameter with special mode "spiralize outer contour". (44 minutes to print) A cylinder 3cm in diameter printed on its side with supports on. (50 minutes to print)
